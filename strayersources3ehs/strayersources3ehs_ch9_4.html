The Birth of a New Religion

AP® EXAM TIP

Pay close attention to these similarities and differences in major religions.

Most of the major religious or cultural traditions of the second-wave era had emerged from the core of established civilizations — Confucianism and Daoism from China, Hinduism and Buddhism from India, Greek philosophy from the Mediterranean world, and Zoroastrianism from Persia. Christianity and Islam, by contrast, emerged more from the margins of Mediterranean and Middle Eastern civilizations. Christianity, of course, appeared among a small Middle Eastern people, the Jews, in a remote province of the Roman Empire, while Islam took hold in the cities and deserts of the Arabian Peninsula.
